Why these pairs?

A pairs with T (or U in RNA) via 2 hydrogen bonds. G pairs with C via 3 hydrogen bonds — making G-C bonds slightly stronger. This complementary pairing ensures genetic information copies accurately.

What is happening?

In DNA replication, the double helix unwinds. Each original strand becomes a template, and free-floating nucleotides snap into their complementary positions to build two identical DNA copies.

The helix shape

DNA's two strands wrap around each other. The zig-zag offset you see here represents how the strands spiral — the classic double helix discovered by Watson and Crick in 1953.
